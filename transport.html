Transport

There are lots of ways for you to explore this magnificent city. All you need to have is the right information before embarking on your journey.

Just be mindful not to run out of credit !

Public Transport

Trams

Trams are a major form of public transport in Melbourne. The tram network is extensive and it is ideal as a means to travel within the center of the city.

You can travel without cost in the free tram zone that spans between the CBD and Docklands.

Learn more

Trains

You can reach further destinations via trains. Most trains operate between the City and a suburban destination via the City Loop, which is a four-track, five station loop that encircles downtown.

As a general rule, trains operate from Flinders Street station to and from their suburban destination via the City loop stations of Southern Cross, Flagstaff, Melbourne Central and Parliament.

Learn more

Buses

There are more routes for buses than trams. They travel further out into the suburbs, and across the city more directly than other public transport options. Moreover, buses run frequently to major hubs, including shopping centres, schools, hospitals, leisure and sports venues, and some of Melbourne's biggest attractions.

Just remember to tap your Myki both on and off when using buses.

Learn more

Myki Card

Myki Money

If you travel occasionally, use myki Money for simple pay-as-you-go service. Just top-up enough money for your whole journey.

For the first 2 hours, you will be charged for only $4.50. If it expires, a day fare applies for just $9.00.

Learn more

Myki Pass

If you travel frequently, consider getting a myki Pass for a better deal. You can buy a myki Pass for 7 days or anywhere between 28 and 365 days.

As an international student, you can buy an annual pass too for just half the price. Now that's a lot of saving.

Learn more
